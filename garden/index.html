A Field Note

The Garden

A patch of the site grown for visitors who were never invited.

What you're looking at

If you're reading this with your own eyes, hello, you're in the right place, and also not the target audience. Somewhere behind the homepage is a little loop of fake pages I built for the crawlers, scrapers, and other automated or not nosy-parkers that come sniffing around for things like login panels, backup files, and anything with "secret" in the name. None of it is real. They just haven't figured that out yet.

How it works

It usually starts wherever a bot's curiosity gets the better of it: something like /bank-information.html, or any other suspiciously promising filename it went hunting for. Wherever it enters, it lands in a short, deliberately pointless loop:

  • bank-information.html

    "almost there... fetching secrets...", then quietly hands off to the garden.

  • garden.html

    "access granted. loading secure resource...", then hands off to the tree.

  • tree.html

    "verifying credentials... please wait...", then back to the bank information.

Round and round it goes. No dead end, no real content, just enough forward momentum to keep something automated clicking through instead of doing whatever it actually came here to do.

The reward, if it earns one

Every so often a crawler asks for something with a bit more promise in the name, secrets.yaml, say. That one does something different. It types itself out like a terminal session, working out your IP, your city, your ISP, logging it all with great seriousness, declaring an "unauthorised access" and a threat level. None of it is real, It's all theatre, running entirely in your browser, storing nothing. and then, once it's done being dramatic, it sends you somewhere very specific on YouTube. You'll know it when you see it.

The cat

None of this is guarded by anything more sophisticated than a mildly unhinged sense of humour. But officially, per the system, every unauthorised access gets escalated straight to response team: one (1) "smol-class" (under 3.5kg) cat (Felis catus domesticus), sharp teeth, estimated response time three to five business days depending on her mood and whether she's currently napping.

I don't write the security policy. She does. She's watching :3.
